The core difference

INTPs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; INFPs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency.

INTP (The Logician) and INFP (The Mediator) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. They are commonly mistaken for each other.

INTP vs INFP: at a glance

  • Energy / I-E: INTP introverts and recharges alone; INFP introverts and recharges alone.
  • Information / N-S: INTP leads with abstraction and patterns; INFP leads with abstraction and patterns.
  • Decisions / T-F: INTP decides on logic first; INFP decides on values first.
  • Lifestyle / J-P: INTP keeps options open; INFP keeps options open.
  • Population: INTP is roughly 3-5%; INFP is roughly 4-5%.

Where they overlap

INTP and INFP share 3 core preferences. They tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they are commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: T/F. INTPs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; INFPs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency. This shows up in leadership style, how they handle conflict, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The decisive tell is the T/F flip. Ask yourself: When you hit a hard call, do you anchor on what is logically correct (T) or on impact on the people involved (F)?

When in doubt, take the 60-second test — it scores word patterns and preference signals rather than abstract self-description.
