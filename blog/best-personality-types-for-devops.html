Best Personality Types for DevOps

Career fit guide · Updated 2026-05-11 · By Brad Kam

Best MBTI types for devops

Answer: The personality types best suited for devops are ISTP, INTJ, INTP, ISTJ, ENTJ. These types' cognitive wiring aligns with what the role actually rewards day-to-day.

  1. ISTP: Pragmatic, hands-on, troubleshoot-first — the archetype SRE.
  2. INTJ: Platform engineering and infrastructure architecture — systems-level thinking.
  3. INTP: Automation engineering — finds the right abstraction and builds the tooling.
  4. ISTJ: Reliability engineering and on-call discipline; processes and runbooks.
  5. ENTJ: Engineering leadership over platform and infrastructure orgs.

What a day looks like in devops

On-call weeks reshape the schedule — pages can come at any hour and the SLA is usually under 15 minutes. Off-call weeks are infrastructure projects: CI/CD improvements, observability, capacity planning, automating away the manual work. Good DevOps work compounds — every hour spent on automation saves 10 hours of toil over the next quarter.

Junior-to-senior progression in devops

Junior SRE/DevOps (1-2 years) runs the playbooks and learns the stack. Senior (3-5 years) owns critical infrastructure and writes the playbooks. Staff (5+) sets technical direction across multiple teams. The split: pure SRE (reliability-focused, lives at scale) vs platform engineering (developer experience, internal tools). Both pay well; the day-to-day is quite different.

What to expect in devops interviews

Linux fundamentals, networking (TCP/IP, DNS, load balancing), one cloud platform deeply, containers and orchestration, Terraform or equivalent IaC. Incident scenarios: 'latency just doubled, walk me through your investigation.' System design at scale. Coding rounds are usually less algorithmic than SWE but still required.
