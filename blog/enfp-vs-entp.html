ENFP vs ENTP: Key Differences

Comparison guide · ENFP (The Campaigner) vs ENTP (The Debater)

The core difference

The key difference between ENFP and ENTP is the T/F axis. ENFPs decide by values first; ENTPs decide by logic first.

ENFP (The Campaigner) and ENTP (The Debater) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. The single-axis flip on T/F produces a noticeably different daily rhythm.

Where they overlap

ENFPs and ENTPs share 3 core preferences. Both tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they're commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: T/F. ENFPs decide by values first; ENTPs decide by logic first. This shows up in leadership style, conflict handling, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The single flip (T/F) is the decisive tell. Ask yourself: When you disagree with someone close to you, does the logic of their argument (T) or the feelings in the room (F) come up first for you?
