Best Personality Types for Writing

Career fit guide · Updated 2026-05-11 · By Brad Kam

Best MBTI types for writing

Answer: The personality types best suited for writing are INFP, INFJ, INTJ, INTP, ENFP. These types' cognitive wiring aligns with what the role actually rewards day-to-day.

  1. INFP: Meaning-making through language; fiction, essays, poetry — writing is their native medium.
  2. INFJ: Long-form nonfiction with psychological depth. Ghostwriting, biography, essay.
  3. INTJ: Structured nonfiction: business, strategy, systems writing. Publishing-track authors.
  4. INTP: Technical writing, philosophy, and deep-dive analysis pieces.
  5. ENFP: Magazine features, creative nonfiction, culture pieces — range + voice.

What a day looks like in writing

Staff writers at publications produce 2-4 pieces a week on tight deadlines. Freelancers manage 5-15 active pitches and assignments. Book authors work to longer drafts with months of solitude. Marketing copywriters produce shorter pieces faster on brief. The work is mostly typing alone, with bursts of editor calls and source interviews.

Junior-to-senior progression in writing

Junior staff (1-3 years) gets assigned topics. Staff writer (3-7) pitches their own. Senior writer / editor (7+) shapes the section or publication. Freelancing is a parallel path — top freelancers can outearn most staff writers but lack benefits and stability. Book publishing is largely separate; advances and royalties vary wildly.

What to expect in writing interviews

Writing samples and clip portfolios. Edit tests (a published piece is given back to you to improve). Pitch meetings — talk through 3-5 ideas you'd want to write. For features writing, source-development and reporting examples matter. For copywriting, brand-voice exercises and short turnaround tests.
