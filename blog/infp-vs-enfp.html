The core difference

INFPs recharges alone and processes internally before speaking; ENFPs recharges with people and thinks out loud.

INFP (The Mediator) and ENFP (The Campaigner) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. They are commonly mistaken for each other.

INFP vs ENFP: at a glance

  • Energy / I-E: INFP introverts and recharges alone; ENFP extroverts and recharges with people.
  • Information / N-S: INFP leads with abstraction and patterns; ENFP leads with abstraction and patterns.
  • Decisions / T-F: INFP decides on values first; ENFP decides on values first.
  • Lifestyle / J-P: INFP keeps options open; ENFP keeps options open.
  • Population: INFP is roughly 4-5%; ENFP is roughly 7-8%.

Where they overlap

INFP and ENFP share 3 core preferences. They tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they are commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: I/E. INFPs recharges alone and processes internally before speaking; ENFPs recharges with people and thinks out loud. This shows up in leadership style, how they handle conflict, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The decisive tell is the I/E flip. Ask yourself: After a big group event, do you feel recharged (E) or drained (I)?

When in doubt, take the 60-second test — it scores word patterns and preference signals rather than abstract self-description.
