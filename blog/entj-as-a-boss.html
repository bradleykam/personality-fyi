ENTJ as a Boss

Updated 2026-05-17 · By Brad Kam

How Commanders manage and lead — their decision-making style, what they reward, where they struggle, and how to work for one effectively.

What it looks like

ENTJ bosses are commanding, fast-decision, and intolerant of inefficiency. They set aggressive targets and expect execution. They reward results visibly and cut underperformers quickly. They prefer face-to-face conflict to email back-and-forth. They make team-wide decisions without consensus when necessary.

Where ENTJs shine in this role

  • Clear strategic direction and aggressive goals
  • Defends team from upward political pressure
  • Promotes results-driven reports quickly

Watch out for

  • Can steamroll quieter team members
  • Impatient with slower-paced colleagues
  • Performance feedback can be harsh in the moment

How to relate to a ENTJ as a boss

Working for an ENTJ: deliver, communicate directly, and don't be precious about feedback. They reward people who take initiative and make decisions. Avoid being the person who needs constant direction — that's the fastest way to lose their respect.
