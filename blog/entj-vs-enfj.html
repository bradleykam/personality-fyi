The core difference

ENTJs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; ENFJs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency.

ENTJ (The Commander) and ENFJ (The Protagonist) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. They are commonly mistaken for each other.

ENTJ vs ENFJ: at a glance

  • Energy / I-E: ENTJ extroverts and recharges with people; ENFJ extroverts and recharges with people.
  • Information / N-S: ENTJ leads with abstraction and patterns; ENFJ leads with abstraction and patterns.
  • Decisions / T-F: ENTJ decides on logic first; ENFJ decides on values first.
  • Lifestyle / J-P: ENTJ closes loops and plans ahead; ENFJ closes loops and plans ahead.
  • Population: ENTJ is roughly 2-5%; ENFJ is roughly 2-3%.

Where they overlap

ENTJ and ENFJ share 3 core preferences. They tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they are commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: T/F. ENTJs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; ENFJs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency. This shows up in leadership style, how they handle conflict, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The decisive tell is the T/F flip. Ask yourself: When you hit a hard call, do you anchor on what is logically correct (T) or on impact on the people involved (F)?

When in doubt, take the 60-second test — it scores word patterns and preference signals rather than abstract self-description.
