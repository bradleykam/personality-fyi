The core difference

INTJs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; INFJs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency.

INTJ (The Architect) and INFJ (The Advocate) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. They are commonly mistaken for each other.

INTJ vs INFJ: at a glance

  • Energy / I-E: INTJ introverts and recharges alone; INFJ introverts and recharges alone.
  • Information / N-S: INTJ leads with abstraction and patterns; INFJ leads with abstraction and patterns.
  • Decisions / T-F: INTJ decides on logic first; INFJ decides on values first.
  • Lifestyle / J-P: INTJ closes loops and plans ahead; INFJ closes loops and plans ahead.
  • Population: INTJ is roughly 2-4%; INFJ is roughly 1-2% (rarest).

Where they overlap

INTJ and INFJ share 3 core preferences. They tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they are commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: T/F. INTJs decides by logic first, evidence before empathy; INFJs decides by values first, impact on people before efficiency. This shows up in leadership style, how they handle conflict, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The decisive tell is the T/F flip. Ask yourself: When you hit a hard call, do you anchor on what is logically correct (T) or on impact on the people involved (F)?

When in doubt, take the 60-second test — it scores word patterns and preference signals rather than abstract self-description.
