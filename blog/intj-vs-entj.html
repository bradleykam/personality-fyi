INTJ vs ENTJ: Key Differences

Comparison guide · INTJ (The Architect) vs ENTJ (The Commander)

The core difference

The key difference between INTJ and ENTJ is the I/E axis. INTJs recharge alone and think before speaking; ENTJs recharge with people and think out loud.

INTJ (The Architect) and ENTJ (The Commander) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. The single-axis flip on I/E produces a noticeably different daily rhythm.

Where they overlap

INTJs and ENTJs share 3 core preferences. Both tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they're commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: I/E. INTJs recharge alone and think before speaking; ENTJs recharge with people and think out loud. This shows up in leadership style, conflict handling, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The single flip (I/E) is the decisive tell. Ask yourself: After a big group event, do you feel recharged (E) or drained (I)?
