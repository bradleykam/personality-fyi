Best Personality Types for Sales

Career fit guide · Updated 2026-05-11 · By Brad Kam

Best MBTI types for sales

Answer: The personality types best suited for sales are ESTP, ENTJ, ENFJ, ENFP, ESFJ. These types' cognitive wiring aligns with what the role actually rewards day-to-day.

  1. ESTP: Reads the room, closes through presence, thrives on leaderboards.
  2. ENTJ: Enterprise/field sales leadership — strategic account management and command presence.
  3. ENFJ: Relationship-first selling through genuine rapport — the high-touch B2B type.
  4. ENFP: High-energy, storytelling, natural connector — great at startup sales and BD.
  5. ESFJ: Warm, service-oriented, remembers every client detail — the retention expert.

What a day looks like in sales

BDRs/SDRs spend 4-6 hours on the phone or in outbound sequences. Account executives mix prospecting, discovery calls, demos, and negotiation. Enterprise reps run multi-month deal cycles with multiple stakeholders per deal. The work is partly emotional regulation — most prospects don't buy, most deals don't close, and you have to keep dialing.

Junior-to-senior progression in sales

BDR/SDR (1-2 years) books meetings. AE (2-4 years) closes deals. Senior AE / Enterprise AE (4-7+) handles strategic accounts. Sales manager runs a team of reps. Director / VP runs multiple teams. The compensation reality: top quartile reps make significantly more than their managers; bottom quartile reps are out within a year. It's the most performance-transparent function in most companies.

What to expect in sales interviews

Mock cold call. Role-play a discovery call. Walk through your last deal — what was hard, what worked, what would you change. Quota attainment history matters. Top tech sales orgs also include analytical rounds — Excel-based deal analysis, forecast accuracy, pipeline math.
