The core difference

ENFJs seeks closure, plans ahead, moves toward resolution; ENFPs keeps options open, prefers exploration over commitment.

ENFJ (The Protagonist) and ENFP (The Campaigner) share 3 of 4 MBTI letters. They are commonly mistaken for each other.

ENFJ vs ENFP: at a glance

  • Energy / I-E: ENFJ extroverts and recharges with people; ENFP extroverts and recharges with people.
  • Information / N-S: ENFJ leads with abstraction and patterns; ENFP leads with abstraction and patterns.
  • Decisions / T-F: ENFJ decides on values first; ENFP decides on values first.
  • Lifestyle / J-P: ENFJ closes loops and plans ahead; ENFP keeps options open.
  • Population: ENFJ is roughly 2-3%; ENFP is roughly 7-8%.

Where they overlap

ENFJ and ENFP share 3 core preferences. They tend to gravitate toward similar environments, similar work, and similar people. To outsiders they can look nearly identical — which is why they are commonly confused.

Where they diverge most

Primary divergence: J/P. ENFJs seeks closure, plans ahead, moves toward resolution; ENFPs keeps options open, prefers exploration over commitment. This shows up in leadership style, how they handle conflict, and how they recover from stress.

How to tell which one you are

The decisive tell is the J/P flip. Ask yourself: Do you find unfinished decisions energizing (P) or exhausting (J)?
