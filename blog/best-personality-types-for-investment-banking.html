Best Personality Types for Investment Banking

Career fit guide · Updated 2026-05-11 · By Brad Kam

Best MBTI types for investment banking

Answer: The personality types best suited for investment banking are ENTJ, INTJ, ESTJ, INTP, ENTP. These types' cognitive wiring aligns with what the role actually rewards day-to-day.

  1. ENTJ: Deal team lead, managing director track — command presence and client-facing strategy.
  2. INTJ: Coverage and advisory work — strategic long-term client relationships.
  3. ESTJ: Process-driven execution; keeps the deal on track through endless iterations.
  4. INTP: Valuation and technical modeling — they find the numbers the deal turns on.
  5. ENTP: Originator / rainmaker role — reading markets and pitching bold angles.

What a day looks like in investment banking

Analyst days are model-build, deck-build, and email — the marathon is 80-100 hours a week including weekends. Associates run the model and review analyst work. The work is intellectually monotonous but operationally intense: you're producing the same materials for different deals, and the bar for accuracy is absolute. A single broken formula in a fairness opinion is a career-defining mistake.

Junior-to-senior progression in investment banking

Analyst (2-3 years) is the grind. Associate (3-4 years) is the bridge. VP (3-4 years) is execution leadership. Director/MD is origination — finding deals. The pyramid is steep: most bankers exit to private equity, hedge funds, or corporate development after analyst or associate. Those who stay are a particular type who can tolerate the hours indefinitely.

What to expect in investment banking interviews

Superdays are 4-6 back-to-back interviews. Expect: walk me through your resume, why banking, why our bank, three technical questions on accounting/valuation/M&A. Stress questions are common — they want to see how you handle pressure. Knowing the firm's recent deals cold is table stakes.
