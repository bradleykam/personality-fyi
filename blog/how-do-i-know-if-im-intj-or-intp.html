How do I know if I'm INTJ or INTP?

Question · Updated 2026-05-17 · By Brad Kam

The short answer

The decisive question is whether you crave closure on decisions (J) or want to keep options open (P). INTJs feel relieved when something is decided; INTPs feel constrained. INTJs build plans they execute; INTPs build frameworks they refine indefinitely.

Signal: how you start a project

Given a new project, INTJs map out the steps, set deadlines, and start executing. INTPs explore the problem space, question whether the project should even exist in its current form, and resist committing until they understand the underlying structure. Both can produce great work; the path is fundamentally different.

Signal: how you handle conflict

INTJs resolve conflict by stating their position clearly and moving on. INTPs detach into analysis to understand why the conflict happened before re-engaging. INTJs enforce boundaries; INTPs disappear and return.

Signal: your desk

INTJs tend toward order; INTPs toward organized chaos. INTJs file; INTPs pile. INTJs maintain calendars; INTPs operate from memory plus reactive scheduling. Either type can override the default, but the natural state is reliably different.

The single tell

When you finish reading a long article you genuinely enjoyed, does your first impulse: (a) close the tab and move on with your day, or (b) immediately open three related articles to keep exploring? (a) leans INTJ; (b) leans INTP. It's the closure-versus-exploration impulse in a small moment.
